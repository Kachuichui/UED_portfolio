Case Study

Exploring Perceptions of AR Technology in the kitchen

Project Details

Role
UX Designer
Duration
2 weeks
Tools
Miro, otter

Overview

This study investigates how people perceive the role of augmented reality (AR) technology in the kitchen and its application in cooking. Based on this purpose, I conducted a 10-minute semi-structured interview.

Problem Statement

Many people encounter various challenges in daily cooking, such as poor time management, excessive cognitive load, and over-reliance on recipes. The original intention of this research is to gain an in-depth understanding of people's perceptions of AR technology in the kitchen, and explore how this technology can truly help them solve these problems, thereby improving the overall cooking experience.

Methodology

To gain a deep understanding of users' thoughts and needs, we adopted the following qualitative research methods:

  1. Semi-structured interviews: 17 college students aged between 21 and 24 were recruited. The interviews lasted 10 minutes, during which we obtained rich and in-depth insights by asking them questions about their cooking habits, perceptions of AR, functional needs, display preferences, etc.
  2. Data analysis: The interview data were analyzed using Affinity Diagrams and Inductive Coding. This process helped us organize the disorganized interview records into meaningful themes and extract key patterns and insights from them.

Interview Structure

The interview followed a basic structure:

  1. I1: Cooking and Baking Habits How frequent, self-assessed cooking level, and difficulties they may have encountered during the cooking or baking process.
  2. I2: AR Awareness Have you heard of augmented reality technology? How do you describe it? If the answer is never heard, the researcher will show them a video and explanation to ensure they know the concept of AR.
  3. I3: Functional Requirements What helpful information would you like AR glasses to provide when cooking or baking?
  4. I4: Display Preferences Where do you think the AR information should be displayed to help you the most without disturbing you?
  5. I5: Recipe Assistance What are your expected AR glasses to assist in providing recipe tips? What are your preferences for different types of guidance, and why?
  6. I6: Workflow Optimization When working in the kitchen, which process best uses AR technology?
  7. I7: Concerns and Suggestions What are your concerns about using AR glasses? Give some suggestions about how to improve.

Key Findings

Through the analysis of interview data, we have identified the following core insights, which provide an important basis for the subsequent design directions:

  1. • Time management is the primary pain point: Most respondents consider managing time during cooking to be a huge challenge, which directly affects the success of cooking and their experience.
  2. • Dynamic rather than fixed information is needed: Users prefer a dynamic and adjustable way of displaying AR information over a fixed interface. For example, they hope that AR information can be placed anywhere they need, just like "sticky notes".
  3. • Video is the preferred form of guidance: When obtaining recipe guidance, video is regarded as the most intuitive and effective form. Users say that videos can provide a more dynamic and immersive cooking experience.
  4. • Visual Design: Created a clean, modern design system aligned with the brand's identity, infused with Tang elements.
  5. • Concerns and expectations about AR coexist: Users express concerns about privacy protection and human comfort of AR technology in the kitchen, but at the same time, they are full of expectations for its potential to help manage the cooking process and provide more personalized guidance.

Design Concept:

Design Concept: Based on the above findings, we proposed and verified a core design concept: "Spatial Timer". This concept aims to use AR technology to present timers and cooking information to users in a spatialized manner, helping them easily manage the time for multiple cooking tasks, thereby significantly reducing cognitive load and improving cooking efficiency. The proposal of this concept effectively transforms research findings into implementable design solutions.

Conclusion & Next Steps

This study conducts interviews to assess how individuals perceive the function of augmented reality technology in the kitchen and its application in cooking. After investigation, I discovered that “time” is a critical influencing factor at the core of several culinary problems. It also indirectly leads to cooking challenges, such as poor time management, which leads to heat control mistakes; preparing ingredients takes too much time, which leads to boredom, etc. Furthermore, time management is the most often reported functional interest. Hence, effective allocation and time management in the cooking scenario provides a significant systemic challenge. It has an impact on the entire cooking process. This discovery enabled the project to present the “spatial timer” concept, which shaped the following evaluation tasks(study2 and study3). Meanwhile, the proposal of this concept has shifted the focus of research. The following studies will evaluate the effect of the “spatial timer” in improving cooking efficiency and reducing cognition based on AR technology’s spatial perception to provide users with a pleasant cooking experience.
