Spatial Timer Prototype

Following the interviews in the previous Study (Study 1), I learned about people’s thoughts on the kitchen AR technology. They struggled with "time" in various culinary tasks. A unique "spatial timer," Inspired by kitchen time management issues, it was created. This page will describe the "spatial timer," its prototype, and the experimental design to evaluate its effectiveness using multiple methods.

Design Inspiration

Traditional timers are often limited to a single device and a fixed location. This makes managing multiple tasks in a dynamic environment, like a kitchen, difficult and inefficient.

To overcome these limitations, I designed a Spatial Timer using Augmented Reality (AR). This transforms the concept of a timer from a 2D display into a 3D virtual object you can place anywhere in your physical space. Key features include:

  • Spatial Mapping: Linking time information directly to specific tasks or locations in the real world.
  • Spatial Interaction: Using natural hand gestures to control timers without touching a device.
  • Multi-Timer Collaboration: Effortlessly creating and managing multiple timers at once to handle complex, parallel tasks.

By bringing time management into our physical space, the Spatial Timer aims to make the process more intuitive, contextual, and efficient.

Spatial Timer Panel Function Time management is Spatial Timer's primary purpose. I concentrated on timer basics and included the following features throughout the design.

  1. • Time Input: A digital keyboard allows minute-level time input. The numeric keypad’s nine panels make numbers easy to find and input.
  2. • Clear Buttons: Users can correct entry errors or alter timer length by clicking Clear.
  3. • Start/Restart buttons:Tap Start to start the countdown and jump to the countdown screen after setting the time. Restarting the countdown starts from scratch.
  4. • Pause/Resume buttons:Clicking the stop and resume buttons pauses and resumes the process.
  5. • Toggle buttons: Users may choose between round and slider countdown panels.
  6. • Delete buttons: Remove timers when no longer required to improve space layout.

Interactive Function Spatial Timer's gesture-based time management is innovative. In a three-dimensional environment, motions make timer generation, manipulation, and management easier. We created these primary gesture interactions:

  1. • Generate Timer: Our gesture was inspired by the British Sign Language "timer" Bsl (2013). Four fingers are bent to the same degree, but the thumb is bent somewhat differently. The user can generate a timer in the surrounding environment by performing this action.
  2. • Shift: Grab and drag movements move the timer anywhere in the space.
  3. • Zooming: Users may instantly reduce or enlarge the timer to fit various spaces using pinch and open actions.
  4. • Rotation: To improve timer spatial orientation, the user may squeeze one of the anchor points to rotate the timer at any angle around its vertical (Y-axis) and horizontal (X-axis) axes.
  5. • Time panel: Users may touch or poke the timer panel buttons to start, stop, reset, and swap timer forms.
